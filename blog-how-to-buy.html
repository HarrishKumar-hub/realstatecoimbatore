How to Buy Land in Coimbatore – Step by Step Guide (2025)

By Sakthi, LPS Real Estate January 2025 8 min read

Buying land in Coimbatore is one of the best investments you can make — but it can be confusing if you don't know the process. After 18+ years of helping families buy land in this region, I've seen all the common mistakes buyers make. This guide covers everything step by step.

Important: Always verify documents with a qualified advocate before making any payment. Never pay in cash without a proper sale agreement.

Step 1 – Define Your Requirements

Before searching, be clear about what you need:

  • Purpose: Home construction, investment, farming, or commercial use?
  • Budget: Include stamp duty (~8% of value) and registration fees in your total budget
  • Size: How many cents or acres do you need?
  • Location: Which areas are within your preferred distance from city/work?
  • Road access: Do you need a 30 ft road? Or is 20 ft enough?

Step 2 – Search for Available Plots

In Coimbatore, you can find plots through:

  • Local brokers with physical site knowledge (like us)
  • Online portals (but verify everything before trusting)
  • Newspaper classified ads
  • Personal referrals from friends and family

Always work with a broker who knows the specific area. Land rates, road quality, flood zones, and soil conditions vary significantly even within the same taluk.

Step 3 – Visit the Site In Person

Never buy land without visiting it physically. During the site visit, check:

  • Road access and road width
  • Neighbouring plots and encroachments
  • Water availability (borewell, river, canal)
  • Electrical line availability
  • Distance from flood zone or government reserve
  • Soil quality (especially for agricultural land)
LPS Real Estate arranges free site visits at your preferred time. We accompany you personally and answer all your questions honestly.

Step 4 – Verify the Documents

This is the most critical step. Hire a qualified advocate to verify:

  • Title Deed / Sale Deed (check the chain of ownership for 30 years)
  • Encumbrance Certificate (EC) from Sub-Registrar – must be clean
  • Patta in the current seller's name
  • DTCP / Panchayat approval letter
  • Survey sketch from government records
  • Property tax receipts (up to date)
  • No government acquisition notice on the survey number

Step 5 – Agree on Price & Sign Sale Agreement

Once documents are clear, negotiate the price. Sign a Sale Agreement (Aaval Pattiram) with:

  • Full description of the land (survey number, boundaries, area)
  • Agreed sale price
  • Advance amount paid
  • Registration date agreed
  • Penalty clauses for either party backing out

The sale agreement must be stamped on non-judicial stamp paper. The advance is usually 10–25% of the total price.

Step 6 – Pay Balance & Register the Property

On the registration day:

  • Both buyer and seller appear at the Sub-Registrar office
  • Two witnesses (with Aadhaar) are required
  • Pay stamp duty (7%) + registration fee (1%) to the government
  • The registered sale deed is issued the same day

Keep the original registered sale deed safely — it is the primary proof of ownership.

Step 7 – Transfer the Patta After Registration

Within 3 months of registration, apply for patta mutation (name change) at the local VAO (Village Administrative Officer) office or online via TN-ePatta portal. This updates government land records to your name.

Summary checklist: Site visit → Document verification → Sale agreement → Full payment & registration → Patta transfer. If you skip any step, problems can arise later.
